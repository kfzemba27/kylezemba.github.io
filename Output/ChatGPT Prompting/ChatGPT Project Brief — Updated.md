An interactive single-page website showcasing a curated collection of AI tools framed specifically for advertising and marketing professionals. ## Tool Card Schema

Every tool entry requires all of these fields:

- **Name**
- **Company**
- **Tagline** — One line, punchy, specific. Not a sentence fragment and not a paragraph.
- **What it is** — 2-3 sentences max. Lead with what it enables, not what it technically does.
- **Best For** — Specific team function. "Creative teams concepting campaign visuals" not "Creatives."
- **Pros** — Sharp, tied to real workflow benefits. No generic filler.
- **Cons** — Honest, practical. Pricing barriers, learning curves, output gaps, limitations.
- **How to use it** — Genuinely actionable. Prompt strategies, workflow tips, feature callouts. Not "sign up and try it."
- **Highlight Features** — What sets it apart from alternatives.
- **Pricing** — Specific tiers with dollar amounts.
- **URL**

## Writing Rules — Non-Negotiable

**Do:**
- Lead with what the tool enables, not what it technically does
- Use active voice throughout
- Name specific advertising and marketing use cases
- Acknowledge trade-offs honestly
- Write like a practitioner briefing a sharp colleague
- Keep "What it is" to 2-3 sentences, no exceptions
- Frame everything through agency workflow lens

**Do not:**
- Use em dashes
- Use exclamation points
- Use buzzwords without substance: "revolutionary," "game-changing," "next-gen," "cutting-edge"
- Use "leverage" as a verb
- Start sentences with "So," or "Basically,"
- Write marketing copy about the tools
- Be sycophantic toward any company
- Pad content to fill space

**Tone:** Practitioner, not pundit. Confident, honest, strategic. Written by someone who has actually used these tools — not a reviewer summarizing press releases.
